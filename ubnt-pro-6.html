Host Adoption Problems Ubiquiti Access Points

Ubnt’s Continuing Decline

After years of faithful and trouble-free service, I opted to replace my Ubiquiti Lite access point with an updated wireless standard and improved features.

It is well known that the latest release to the Ubiquiti controller software has not been well received. Most of the uproar seemed centered on the implementation of promotion of Ubiquiti’s products on the main console. But my problem with it is the controller software is that the product is just buggy. Design choices seem centered on eye candy and “latest” rather than a functioning product.

The metrics are mixed bag with some improvements, but UI elements are not updating properly and changes made in JS elements can have displays that are horribly out of date. The killer though is, the software absolutely fails at adopting devices.

The controller software absolutely fails at adopting ALL devices, regardless of firmware revision.

Forcing Adoption

Forcing adoption in the UI fails, the only way to force adoption is to ssh into the access point and in a repeated loop, enter

set-inform http://[controller-ip]:8080/inform

Then, click the adoption button.

On the Pro-6, this required getting even more medieval by restarting the controller software, and then putting the inform command into a loop.

for n in {1..30} ; do set-inform http://[controller-ip]:8080/inform ; sleep 1 ; done

While that is executing, hitting the adopt button for the device on the controller UI seems to eventually pick it up.
